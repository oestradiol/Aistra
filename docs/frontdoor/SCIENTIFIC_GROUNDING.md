# Scientific grounding

Aistra is not just a software/testing package. It also includes a research-grounding layer that explains why these fields and route designs were chosen and what evidence supports them.

## What that grounding does
It gives a bounded scientific rationale for:
- why these fields were chosen
- why Route 1 and Route 2 are separated
- why scoring and field weights are provisional rather than final
- what the project can reasonably claim at its current stage

## What it does not do
It does not turn the package into finished science or erase the fact that Aistra is still a prototype.
It does not replace broader human validation.
It does not make the ontology final.

## Honest summary
The research layer is there to keep the representation aligned with real literature and explicit reasoning. The package layer is there to make the representation testable and reproducible. Both are needed.


The research-grounding layer is the scientific alignment surface. Engineering validators can prove internal consistency and reproducibility, but the research-grounding files carry the actual scientific rationale and boundary logic for the field set and pilot posture. Those validators are not psychometric validation by themselves.
