# Participant instructions — Route 1 pilot v0.1

## What this is
You are helping test a small experimental system for describing short bodily-affective states.

This is **not** a diagnostic test and there are no right answers about you as a person.
The task is only about how understandable the current system is.

## What you will do
You will complete:
- 2 practice items
- 8 scored items

Some items will show:
- a structured encoded string

Other items will show:
- a short plain-language description

For each item, your job is to fill out the response form using the categories provided.

## Important
- Work with your best interpretation.
- Do not overthink.
- If something feels unclear, choose the closest option and use the comments box.
- During practice, you may ask clarifying questions.
- During scored items, no corrective feedback will be given.

## What to avoid
- Do not try to memorize theory.
- Do not worry about being perfect.
- Do not try to game the score.

## What we want to learn
We want to know:
- which parts are easy
- which parts are confusing
- whether the current categories feel usable
- whether the structured form behaves differently from ordinary language

## After the task
You will be asked:
- what was hard
- what felt missing
- which parts felt natural or unnatural
